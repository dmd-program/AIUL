AI Usage License (AIUL) Framework for Digital Arts Courses

Faculty can select one of the following AI Usage License (AIUL) levels to include in their syllabi. Each level has a short code for easy reference, similar to Creative Commons.

1. No AI Usage Permitted

AIUL-0
"AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Midjourney, Runway, DALLÂ·E) are not permitted in this course for any part of the assignments. All work must be fully created by the student without AI assistance. Using AI tools may result in academic integrity violations."
Copied!

2. Limited AI Assistance (Instructor Pre-Approval Required)

AIUL-1
"AI tools may be used only with explicit instructor permission and must be documented in your process. If AI is used, students must include a statement describing how it was used. Unauthorized use of AI tools may result in grade penalties."
Copied!

3. AI for Research & Inspiration Only

AIUL-2
"AI tools may be used for brainstorming, research, and idea generation only (e.g., gathering visual references, summarizing sources, or exploring themes). However, AI-generated content (text, images, audio, video, 3D models, etc.) may not be directly used in the final assignment. Students must cite AI sources when applicable."
Copied!

4. AI as a Collaborative Tool (Student-Created Work Required)

AIUL-3
"AI tools may be used as a supporting tool for refining work, generating ideas, and assisting with technical aspects (e.g., grammar correction, color palette suggestions, audio enhancement). However, students must significantly contribute to and refine the final work beyond AI outputs. Students must document and credit their AI usage."
Copied!

5. AI-Assisted Creation Permitted (Student as Director)

AIUL-4
"Students may incorporate AI-generated elements into their projects (e.g., AI-generated text, music, images, or animations), as long as they significantly modify or build upon them. The student should act as the creative director, making key artistic choices. All AI contributions must be clearly cited."
Copied!

6. AI-Integrated Assignments (Required AI Usage)

AIUL-5
"This course includes assignments that require the use of AI tools for creation, experimentation, or critique. Students will explore AI-generated content as part of their creative process and will be expected to document their methods and reflect on AI's role in digital arts."
Copied!

Implementation Notes

  • Faculty can modify these statements to fit their courses.
  • Consider adding an AI usage disclosure requirement for transparency.
  • Similar to Creative Commons, faculty can list "AIUL-[level]" in their syllabus or assignment sheets to easily communicate the policy.
